Suppose
\begin{equation*} A = \left[\begin{array}{cc} -1 \amp -3\cr 0 \amp 2 \end{array}\right]. \end{equation*}
Find an invertible matrix \(P\) and a diagonal matrix \(D\) so that \(A = P D P^{-1}\text{.}\) Use your answer to find an expression for \(A^{7}\) in terms of \(P\text{,}\) a power of \(D\text{,}\) and \(P^{-1}\) in that order.
