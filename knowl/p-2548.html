To compute the dimension of a vector space \(V\) proceed as follows.
  1. Basis.
    Attempt to produce a basis of \(V\text{,}\) possibly with the help of the by-inspection basis technique.
  2. Dimension.
    • If you found a finite basis \(B\) in Step 1, then determine the cardinality \(n=\abs{B}\) by counting the number of distinct elements of \(B\text{.}\) We have \(\dim V=n\) in this case.
    • If you found an infinite basis in Step 1, or were able to show that no finite basis exists, then \(\dim V=\infty\text{.}\)
