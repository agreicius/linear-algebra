CorollaryÂ 3.7.13 is also of use in situations where we wish to characterize all subspaces of a given \(n\)-dimensional vector space \(V\text{.}\) By the corollary, any such subspace \(W\) must have dimension \(k\) for some \(0\leq k\leq n\text{;}\) and this in turn means that \(W\) must have a basis of cardinality \(k\text{.}\) We thus have the beginnings of a systematic description of all subspaces \(W\subseteq V\text{,}\) organized by dimension \(k=\dim W\text{,}\) for \(0\leq k\leq n\text{.}\)
