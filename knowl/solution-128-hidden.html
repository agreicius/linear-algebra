Solution: (a) The idea is to find a convenient point lying in the given plane. For example, let us pick \(P_1 = (1,0,1)\text{,}\) and let \(\mathbf{v}\) be the vector from \(P_1\) to \(P_0\text{.}\) Then the distance from \(P_0\) to the plane is the absolute value of the scalar projection of \(\mathbf{v}\) onto the normal vector
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{N} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 2 \amp 4 \amp -7 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
So, we get that the distance is \(\frac{13}{\sqrt{69}}\text{.}\)
(b) To find an equation of the plane in question, we can work with the point \(P\) and the normal vector \(\mathbf{N}\) given by the cross product of the vector from \(P\) to \(Q\text{,}\) and the vector from \(P\) to \(R\text{.}\) It turns out that
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{N} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} -13 \amp -46 \amp -15 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
So, an equation of the plane is \(-13 x - 46 y - 15 z= 49\text{.}\)
