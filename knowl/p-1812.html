Fix a nonzero vector \(\boldv\in \R^n\text{,}\) where \(n=2\) or \(n=3\text{,}\) and let \(\ell\) be the line obtained by taking all scalar multiples of \(\boldv\text{.}\) The orthogonal projection function \(\operatorname{proj}_\ell\colon \R^n\rightarrow \R^n\) is a linear transformation. In fact, we have \(\operatorname{proj}_\ell=T_A\) where \(A\) is the \(n\times n\) matrix described below for each case (\(n=2\) and \(n=3\)).
  • Case: \(n=2\).
    Assume \(\boldv=(a,b)\ne (0,0)\text{.}\) We have \(\operatorname{proj}_\ell=T_A\text{,}\) where
    \begin{equation*} A=\frac{1}{a^2+b^2}\begin{bmatrix}a^2\amp ab \\ ab \amp b^2 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  • Case: \(n=3\).
    Assume \(\boldv=(a,b,c)\ne (0,0,0)\text{.}\) We have \(\operatorname{proj}_\ell=T_A\text{,}\) where
    \begin{equation*} A=\frac{1}{a^2+b^2+c^2}\begin{bmatrix}a^2\amp ab\amp ac\\ ab \amp b^2 \amp bc \\ ac\amp bc\amp c^2 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
