Let \(\boldn=(a,b,c)\) be a nonzero vector of \(\R^3\text{,}\) and let \(W\) be the plane passing through the origin with normal vector \(\boldn\text{.}\) The orthogonal projection map \(\operatorname{proj}_W\colon \R^3\rightarrow \R^3\) is a linear transformation. In fact, we have \(\operatorname{proj}_W=T_A\) where
\begin{equation} A=\frac{1}{a^2+b^2+c^2}\begin{amatrix}[rrr] b^2+c^2\amp -ab \amp -ac \\ -ab \amp a^2+c^2 \amp -bc \\ -ac \amp -bc \amp a^2+b^2 \end{amatrix}\text{.}\tag{3.2.5} \end{equation}
