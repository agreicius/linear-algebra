Remark 4.5.28.

For the characteristic polynomial of a linear transformation \(T\colon V\rightarrow V\) to be well-defined, it should not depend on the choice of basis. This is true thanks to Theorem 4.5.21 and Theorem 4.3.13. Indeed, given two choice of ordered bases \(B, B'\) of \(V\text{,}\) the matrices \(A=[T]_B\) and \(A'=[T]_{B'}\) are similar (4.3.13), and thus their characteristic polynomials are equal (4.5.21,(2)).
