Theorem 2.3.3. Cancellation with invertible matrices.

Suppose \(Q\) is an invertible \(n\times n\) matrix.
  1. Left-cancellation.
    Given \(n\times r\) matrices \(X\) and \(Y\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} QX=QY \iff X=Y\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Right-cancellation.
    Given \(m\times n\) matrices \(X\) and \(Y\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} XQ=YQ \iff X=Y\text{.} \end{equation*}
