Let
\begin{equation*} \vec{x} = \left[\begin{array}{c} 4\cr -3\cr -3\cr 0 \end{array}\right], \ \vec{y} = \left[\begin{array}{c} -7\cr 3\cr -1\cr 1 \end{array}\right], \ \vec{z} = \left[\begin{array}{c} -8.5\cr -14\cr 14\cr 17.5 \end{array}\right]. \end{equation*}
Use the Gram-Schmidt process to determine an orthonormal basis for the subspace of \({\mathbb R}^4\) spanned by \(\vec{x}\text{,}\) \(\vec{y}\text{,}\) and \(\vec{z}\text{.}\)
