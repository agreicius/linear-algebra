• Since each \(A_i\) is symmetric, and since the set of symmetric matrices \(W'\) is a subspace, we have \(W=\Span\{A_1, A_2,A_3\}\subseteq W'\) by statement (2) of Theorem 3.5.5.
  • The set \(B=\{A_1, A_2, A_3\}\) is linearly independent:
    \begin{align*} aA_1+bA_2+cA_3=\boldzero \amp \iff \begin{bmatrix} a\amp a+b+c\\ a+b+c\amp c \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 0\amp 0\\ 0\amp 0 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp \iff a=c=0 \text{ and } a+b+c=0 \\ \amp \iff a=b=c=0\text{.} \end{align*}
    Thus \(B\) is a basis of \(W=\Span B\text{,}\) and we conclude that \(\dim W=3\text{.}\)
  • We saw in Example 3.7.9 that \(\dim W'=3\text{:}\) i.e., the space of symmetric \(2\times 2\) is \(3\)-dimensional. Since \(W\) is a subspace of \(W'\) and \(\dim W=\dim W'=3\text{,}\) we conclude that \(W=W'\) by Corollary 3.7.13.
