Example 3.7.8. Subspace of \(P_3\).

Let \(W\subseteq P_3\) be the subspace of polynomials whose leading coefficient is equal to their constant term: i.e.,
\begin{equation*} W=\{a_3x^3+a_2x^2+a_1x+a_0\colon a_3=a_0\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Compute \(\dim W\) using ProcedureÂ 3.7.6. You may take for granted that \(W\) is a subspace.

Solution.
First we use ProcedureÂ 3.6.13 to produce a basis of \(W\text{.}\) A general element of \(W\) has parametric description
\begin{equation} p(x)=ax^3+bx^2+cx+a=a(x^3+1)+bx^2+cx\text{,}\tag{3.22} \end{equation}
from which we see immediately that \(B=\{x^3+1, x^2, x\}\) is a spanning set of \(W\text{.}\) From (3.22) we also see that
\begin{align*} a(x^3+1)+bx^2+cx=\boldzero \amp \iff ax^3+bx^2+cx+a=0x^3+0x^2+0x+0 \\ \amp \iff a=b=c=0\text{.} \end{align*}
Thus \(B\) is a basis of \(W\text{.}\) Since \(B\) contains three distinct elements, we conclude that \(\dim W=\abs{B}=3\text{.}\)
