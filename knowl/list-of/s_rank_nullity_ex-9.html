Solution.
  1. Clearly, \(B=\{(1,1)\}\) is a basis for \(\CS A\text{,}\) and \(B'=\{(1,1,1,1)\}\) is a basis for \(\RS A\text{.}\) It follows that \(\rank A=1\) and hence \(\nullity A=4-1=3\text{.}\) Thus we need to find three linearly independent elements of \(\NS A\) to find a basis. We can do so by inspection with the help of the column method. Namely, observe that \(\boldv_1=(1,-1,0,0), \boldv_2=(0,1,-1,0), \boldv_3=(0,0,1,-1)\) are all in \(\NS A\) (column method). The location of zeros in these vectors make it clear that \(B''=\{\boldv_1,\boldv_2, \boldv_3\}\) are linearly independent. Since \(\dim NS A=3\text{,}\) and \(\val{B''}=3\text{,}\) we conclude that \(B''\) is a basis of \(\NS A\) (3.7.13).
