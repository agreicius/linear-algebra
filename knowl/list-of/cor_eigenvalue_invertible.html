Corollary 4.4.22. Null space as eigenspace.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix.
  1. The \(0\)-eigenspace of \(A\) is equal to the null space of \(A\text{:}\) i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} W_0=\NS A\text{.} \end{equation*}

Proof.

  1. We have
    \begin{equation*} W_0=\NS(0I-A)=\NS(-A). \end{equation*}
    Since \(-A\boldx=\boldzero\) if and only if \(A\boldx=\boldzero\text{,}\) we conclude that \(\NS(-A)=\NS A\text{,}\) and hence \(W_0=\NS A\text{.}\)
