Theorem 5.1.9. Dot product and matrix multiplication.

  1. Given \(n\)-tuples \(\boldx=(x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\) and \(\boldy=(y_1,y_2,\dots, y_n)\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{align*} \boldx\cdot \boldy \amp = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \amp x_2\amp \dots \amp x_n\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} y_1\\ y_2\\ \vdots \\ y_n \end{bmatrix} \\ \amp=\boldx^T\boldy \text{,} \end{align*}
    where in the last equality \(\boldx\) and \(\boldy\) are treated as column vectors.

  2. Let \(A\) be an \(m\times n\) matrix, and let \(B\) be a \(n\times r\) matrix. Let \(\boldr_i\in \R^n\) be the \(i\)-th row of \(A\text{,}\) and let \(\boldc_j\in \R^n\) be the \(j\)-th column of \(B\text{.}\) For all \(1\leq i\leq m, 1\leq j\leq n\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} (AB)_{ij}=\boldr_i\cdot \boldc_j\text{,} \end{equation*}
    where \(\boldr_i\) and \(\boldc_j\) are considered as \(n\)-tuples. In other words, the \(ij\)-th entry of \(AB\) is the dot product of the \(i\)-th row of \(A\) and the \(j\)-th column of \(B\text{.}\)
