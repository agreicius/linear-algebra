Definition 0.7.1. Polynomials.

Assume \(X\subseteq \C\) a polynomial on \(X\) is a function \(f\) of the form
\begin{equation} f(x)=\sum_{i=1}^\infty a_ix^i\text{,}\tag{0.7} \end{equation}
where \(a_i\in \C\) for all \(i\text{,}\) and there is a positive integer \(n\) such that \(a_i=0\) for all \(i> n\text{.}\) Equivalently, \(f\) is a function of the form
\begin{equation} f(x)=\anpoly\text{,}\tag{0.8} \end{equation}
where \(a_i\in \C\) for all \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\)

We call \(a_ix^i\) the \(i\)-th term of \(f\text{,}\) and \(a_i\) the \(i\)-th coefficient; \(a_0\) is called the constant term of \(f\text{.}\) Furthermore, if in the expression (0.8) we have \(a_n\ne 0\text{,}\) then \(a_nx^{n}\) is called the leading term of \(f\text{,}\) and \(a_{n}\) its leading coefficient. Lastly, a root of \(f\) is a an element \(c\in X\) such that \(f(c)=0\text{.}\)
