Theorem 2.5.17. Adjoint matrix formula.

Given an \(n\times n\) matrix \(A\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} A\,(\adj A)=(\adj A)\, A=(\det A)I\text{.}\tag{2.5.7} \end{equation}
As a consequence, if \(\det A\ne 0\text{,}\) then \(A\) is invertible and
\begin{equation} A^{-1}=\frac{1}{\det A}\, \adj A\text{.}\tag{2.5.8} \end{equation}

Proof.

First observe that the second statement regarding invertibility follows directly from (2.5.7), since in this case setting \(B=\frac{1}{\det A}\, \adj A\) we have
\begin{equation*} AB=BA=\frac{1}{\det A}(A\adj A)=\frac{1}{\det A}((\det A)\, I)=I\text{.} \end{equation*}
