Theorem 4.5.21. Properties of similarity.

Suppose \(A\) is similar to \(A'\text{:}\) i.e., there is an invertible matrix \(P\) such that \(A'=P^{-1}AP\text{.}\) The following hold:
  1. \(A'\) is similar to \(A\text{:}\) i.e., there is an invertible matrix \(Q\) such that \(A=Q^{-1}AQ\text{.}\)

Proof.

Statement (1) follows by taking \(Q=P^{-1}\text{.}\)

Let \(p_A(t)\) and \(p_{A'}(t)\) be the characteristic polynomials of \(A\) and \(A'\text{,}\) repsectively. We have
\begin{align*} p_{A'}(t)\amp =\det(tI-A')\\ \amp =\det(tI-P^{-1}AP) \amp (A'=P^{-1}AP)\\ \amp = \det(P^{-1}tIP-P^{-1}AP) \amp (\text{algebra}) \\ \amp = \det(P^{-1}(tI-A)P) \amp (\text{left/right dist.}) \\ \amp = \det(P^{-1})\det(tI-A)\det(P) \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_det_mult.html}{\text{2.5.26}}) \\ \amp = (\det(P))^{-1}\det(P)\det(tI-A) \\ \amp = \det(tI-A)=p_A(t)\text{.} \end{align*}
This proves statement (2).
