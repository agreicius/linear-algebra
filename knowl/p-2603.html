If \(\dim W=1\text{,}\) then \(W\) has a basis \(B=\{\boldv\}\) consisting of a single nonzero vector \(\boldv\in \R^3\text{.}\) In this case we have \(W=\Span\{\boldv\}=\{t\boldv\colon t\in \R\}\text{,}\) which is a line passing through the origin with direction vector \(\boldv\text{.}\) Thus \(1\)-dimensional subspaces of \(\R^3\) are lines passing through the origin.
