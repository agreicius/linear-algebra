The following corollary follows from Theorem 3.7.3 and Theorem 3.7.11. We call it a “street smarts” result as the first two statements give us a quick and dirty way of dashing a set’s hopes of being a basis. The third statement asserts that when a finite set’s cardinality matches the dimension of a space, then to prove it is a basis it suffices to prove either one of the two defining properties of Definition 3.6.1.
