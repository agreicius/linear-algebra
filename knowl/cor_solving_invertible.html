Corollary 2.3.4. Solving with invertible matrices.

Suppose \(Q\) is an invertible \(n\times n\) matrix.
  1. If \(X\) and \(Y\) are \(n\times r\) matrices, then
    \begin{equation*} QX=Y \iff X=Q^{-1}Y\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. If \(X\) and \(Y\) are \(m\times n\) matrices, then
    \begin{equation*} XQ=Y \iff X=YQ^{-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Proof.
